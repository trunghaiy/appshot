# Productivity / Task Management — Appshot Video Strategy

## Pain Points

- Scattered across 5 different apps — tasks in one, notes in another, calendar elsewhere
- Context switching kills focus — every app switch costs 23 minutes to refocus
- Important tasks buried under noise — can't see what actually matters today
- Capture friction — by the time you open the app, you forgot the thought
- Planning paralysis — spending more time organizing than doing

## Recommended Narrative Arc

**Hook-Demo-Proof** — skip the pain-point scene. Everyone knows productivity apps and the problems they solve. Lead with the clean, calm UI (the design IS the selling point). Show how fast it works, then prove it with stats.

Scene order: feature-showcase, speed-demo, social-proof, call-to-action.

## Scene-by-Scene Guidance

### feature-showcase
- **Caption examples:** "Everything in one place" / "Your day, at a glance"
- **Card suggestions:** "Today" view with 3-4 prioritized tasks, clean inbox with zero unread, integrated notes panel. Keep it minimal and calm — whitespace is the message.

### speed-demo
- **Caption examples:** "Capture a task in 2 seconds" / "Thought to task, instantly"
- **Core action to demonstrate:** typing a task like "Review Q3 report" or "Call dentist" and watching it land in the right place automatically.
- **Suggested typing speed:** start at frame 10, 4-frame duration per character for a snappy feel.

### social-proof
- **Caption examples:** "Your system, working for you" / "From scattered to streamlined"
- **Stat value/label suggestions:** "1,247 tasks completed" or "32 projects shipped" — pick whichever the app tracks.
- **Timeline milestones:** "Week 1: Getting Organized" then "Week 4: In Flow" then "Week 12: System on Autopilot"

### call-to-action
- **Tagline + highlight:** "One place for everything" + "that stays out of your way."
- **Pill suggestions:** ["Instant Capture", "Focus Mode"] or ["Smart Lists", "Zero Inbox"]

## Proof Metrics

Tasks completed, projects shipped, time saved per week, inbox zero streaks, focus session duration. Pick metrics that show momentum and consistency, not just raw counts.

## Emotional Hook Patterns

Calm and clarity — "Finally, one place for everything." The core feeling is relief: the mental load is gone. Secondary hooks: competence and getting things done (the user is effective, not just busy), simplicity as luxury (less UI means more trust), and confidence in a system that won't let things slip.
