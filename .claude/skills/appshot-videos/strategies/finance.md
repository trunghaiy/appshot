# Finance / Budgeting — Appshot Video Strategy

## Pain Points

- No idea where money goes each month — surprise overdrafts
- Spreadsheet fatigue — tracking expenses manually is unsustainable
- Subscription creep — small charges adding up unnoticed
- Anxiety about checking bank balance — avoidance behavior
- Partner disagreements about spending — no shared visibility

## Recommended Narrative Arc

**Problem-Solution-Proof** — "Where did it all go?" is a universal, visceral pain. Lead with the anxiety of not knowing, then show the clarity your app provides. The problem is felt before it is understood, which makes the recognition moment powerful.

## Scene-by-Scene Guidance

### pain-point
- **Caption:** "Where did it all go?"
- **Headline:** "Monthly Budget"
- **Counter:** Balance draining from $2,400 to $0 over the scene duration. Use a currency counter that ticks down steadily — viewers feel the loss in real time.

### feature-showcase
- **Caption:** "See every dollar, instantly"
- **Card suggestions:**
  - Clean spending dashboard with category breakdown (food, transport, subscriptions)
  - Budget progress bars showing remaining vs. spent
  - Subscription tracker surfacing forgotten charges

### speed-demo
- **Caption:** "Track spending in 3 seconds"
- **Core action:** Logging a single expense — show a value like "Coffee $4.50" or "Groceries $62.10" being entered and categorized automatically.
- **Typing speed:** Start at frame 15, duration 20 frames. Keep it brisk — the whole point is effortless logging.

### social-proof
- **Caption:** "Saved $2,400 this year"
- **Stat value/label:** "$2,400 saved" or "47 subscriptions found"
- **Timeline milestones:**
  - Month 1: Awareness
  - Month 3: In Control
  - Month 6: Building Wealth

### call-to-action
- **Tagline:** "Know where your money goes"
- **Highlight:** "without the spreadsheet."
- **Pills:** ["Instant Logging", "Smart Categories"]

## Proof Metrics

Money saved per year, total spending tracked, budget adherence percentage, subscriptions found and cancelled, days of streak maintained. Dollar figures resonate most — abstract percentages are weaker unless paired with a concrete amount.

## Emotional Hook Patterns

Control and relief — "Finally know where it goes." Anxiety reduction is the primary driver; the shift from dread to clarity. Secondary hooks: partner harmony (shared budgets ending arguments), compound growth (small savings becoming real wealth over time), and identity shift from "bad with money" to "in control."
