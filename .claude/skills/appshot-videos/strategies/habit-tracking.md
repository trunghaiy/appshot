# Habit Tracking — Appshot Video Strategy

Apps for building habits, maintaining streaks, tracking daily routines, journaling, or self-improvement.

## Pain Points

- All-or-nothing streaks: miss one day, lose everything — feels punishing
- Shame spiral after a missed day — app makes you feel worse, not better
- No visible progress — you do the work but can't see the compounding effect
- Willpower fatigue — relying on motivation instead of systems
- App overload — too many trackers, none of them stick

## Recommended Narrative Arc

**Transformation** — pain-point → feature-showcase → social-proof → speed-demo → call-to-action

Habit apps sell identity change, not features. The viewer needs to feel the pain of broken streaks, see the compassionate alternative, witness the identity transformation ("Curious Reader → Consistent Reader"), then see how fast and easy it is. The "speed" proof is more powerful after they already want the outcome.

## Scene-by-Scene Guidance

### pain-point
- **Caption examples:** "Every other app does this." / "Miss one day. Start over." / "Sound familiar?"
- **Headline:** "Daily Streak" or the habit type ("Reading Streak", "Workout Streak")
- **Items/counter:** 14 filled dots + 1 empty = the break. Counter 14 → 0 shows total loss. The bigger the starting number, the more painful the loss feels. Use 14 (two weeks of work destroyed) as a baseline.

### feature-showcase
- **Caption examples:** "[App] keeps your streak alive." / "Consistency, not perfection."
- **Card suggestions:**
  1. Identity card: "You're becoming a [identity]" with a streak highlight ("12-week streak")
  2. Weekly view: day-of-week dots (M T W T F S S) showing 5/7 active — imperfect but consistent
  3. Optional: recovery/forgiveness feature card if the app has one

### social-proof
- **Caption examples:** "Watch yourself become a [identity]." / "Your progress, visualized."
- **Stat:** comparative works well — "Top 12% of [app] users" (aspirational but achievable)
- **Timeline milestones:**
  - Week 1: "Curious [Identity]" (starting)
  - Week 4: "Habit Builder" (forming the habit)
  - Week 12: "Consistent [Identity]" (identity cemented)

  These map to real habit formation research: ~21 days to form, ~66 days to automate.

### speed-demo
- **Caption examples:** "Log [action] in 5 seconds." / "The fastest way to [action]."
- **Core action:** the minimal daily interaction (log a page number, check off a habit, record a session)
- **Typing speed:** start at 0.6s, type over 0.3s — feels quick but readable

### call-to-action
- **Tagline + highlight:** "Build a [habit]" + "that survives missed days." — the highlight should directly answer the pain point
- **Pills:** "Flexible Streaks" + "Compassionate Recovery" — name the differentiators that competitors don't offer

## Proof Metrics

- Streak length (days/weeks of consistency)
- Consistency rate (% of days completed — better than streak because it doesn't punish misses)
- Identity milestone progression
- Community comparison ("Top X%")

## Emotional Hook Patterns

**Identity transformation.** The user doesn't just want to read — they want to *become a reader*. Frame everything as identity, not behavior. "You're becoming a consistent reader" hits harder than "You've read 12 books."

**Compassion over punishment.** The key differentiator for modern habit apps: missed days don't destroy progress. The video should show that the "old way" punishes, while your app forgives.
