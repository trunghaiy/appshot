# Fitness / Workout / Health — Appshot Video Strategy

## Pain Points

- Miss one gym day, streak resets to zero — all motivation gone
- Complex calorie/macro counting that takes longer than the meal itself
- Can't see progress — doing the work but no visible change over weeks
- App overload: separate apps for workouts, nutrition, steps, and sleep
- Generic programs that don't adapt to your schedule or fitness level

## Recommended Narrative Arc

**Problem-Solution-Proof** — lead with streak loss or complexity frustration. Fitness users feel the pain viscerally; they have all lost a gym streak or abandoned a plan that felt too rigid. The dark crack animation maps perfectly to "miss one day, lose everything." Follow immediately with the flexible alternative, then prove it with consistency stats.

## Scene-by-Scene Guidance

### pain-point
- **Caption examples:** "Miss one day. Lose everything." / "Sound familiar?"
- **Headline:** "Workout Streak"
- **Items/counter:** Streak counter animating from 30 down to 0. Calendar squares filling green then shattering. A progress bar draining to empty.

### feature-showcase
- **Caption examples:** "Finally, a plan that bends — not breaks." / "Recovery built in."
- **Card suggestions:**
  - Workout calendar with flexible recovery days that shift automatically
  - Progressive overload cards showing weight/rep targets adjusting per session
  - Unified dashboard combining workouts, nutrition, and sleep in one view

### speed-demo
- **Caption examples:** "Log a workout in 4 seconds." / "One tap. Done."
- **Core action to demonstrate:** Logging a completed exercise — type a value like "Bench Press" or a weight like "185" into the search/input field, then tap to confirm.
- **Suggested typing speed:** Start around frame 15, type over 18-22 frames for a snappy feel.

### social-proof
- **Caption examples:** "Top 5% consistency." / "Watch your numbers climb."
- **Stat value/label suggestions:** "94% consistency" or "12-week streak" — pick whichever the app tracks.
- **Timeline milestones:**
  - Week 1: "Getting Started"
  - Week 4: "Building Momentum"
  - Week 8: "Unstoppable"

### call-to-action
- **Tagline + highlight:** "Build the body" + "without the burnout."
- **Pill suggestions:** ["Smart Recovery", "No Zero Days", "Adaptive Plans"]

## Proof Metrics

Consistency percentage, streak length (days/weeks), total workouts completed, personal records hit, weight progression over time. Consistency is the strongest — it reframes success from "perfect attendance" to "showing up most of the time."

## Emotional Hook Patterns

- **Identity transformation:** "Become someone who never misses" — shift from doing workouts to being a consistent person.
- **Progress visibility:** "Watch your numbers climb" — concrete, undeniable proof via charts and PRs.
- **Community belonging:** "Top X%" rankings that create aspirational peer pressure.
- **Relief:** "No more guilt for rest days" — permission-based messaging that lowers resistance to starting.
