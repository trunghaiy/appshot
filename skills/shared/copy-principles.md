# Copy Principles for App Store Assets

These principles apply to all text in preview videos and screenshots. Read this before drafting any captions, headlines, taglines, or pills.

## Core Rules

1. **Billboard test.** Every line must be readable at a glance. Max 8 words for video captions, max 6 words for screenshot headlines.

2. **Benefit-first.** Lead with what the user gets, not what the app does. "Log any book in 5 seconds" beats "Quick page entry feature."

3. **One job per line.** Each text element does exactly one thing:
   - **Provoke** — trigger recognition of a problem ("Every other app does this")
   - **State** — declare a benefit or fact ("Log any book in 5 seconds")
   - **Call to action** — reduce friction and invite ("Free to start. Your streak is waiting.")

4. **Be specific.** Include numbers, names, or concrete outcomes. "Save 2 hours a week" beats "Save time." "Top 12% of readers" beats "Join the community."

5. **No jargon.** Write for someone who has never heard of your app. If a word only makes sense to existing users, cut it.

## Tagline Formula

Two-part structure: **setup** + **differentiator**.

- Setup: the benefit category (what you do)
- Differentiator: what makes you different (goes in `taglineHighlight`, renders in brand color)

Examples:
- "Build a reading habit" + **"that survives missed days."**
- "Track your spending" + **"without the spreadsheet."**
- "Remember every workout" + **"in under 5 seconds."**

The differentiator should answer: "Why this app instead of the 50 others?"

## Pills / Feature Badges

- 2-3 pills maximum. More dilutes the message.
- Each pill is a **benefit**, not a feature name. "Flexible Streaks" not "Streak System." "Smart Recovery" not "Recovery Mode."
- Pills should **differentiate** — what do you do that competitors don't? Generic benefits ("Easy to Use", "Beautiful Design") waste the slot.

## Medium-Specific Guidance

### Video Captions
- Transient — the viewer reads while watching motion. Keep shorter (5-8 words).
- Each caption advances the narrative. The sequence should tell a story even without the visuals.
- Pain-point caption: triggers recognition ("Sound familiar?" / "We've all been there")
- Feature caption: names the solution with confidence ("[App] makes it effortless")
- Speed-demo caption: must include a specific time or number ("in 5 seconds" / "in 3 taps")
- Social-proof caption: invokes identity or progress ("Watch yourself become a reader")
- CTA caption: reduces friction + creates gentle urgency ("Free to start. Your streak is waiting.")

### Screenshot Headlines
- Permanent — the viewer reads at leisure in the store listing. Can be slightly bolder.
- 4-6 words max. One headline per screenshot.
- Headlines must be **scannable as a sequence** — reading all headlines top-to-bottom tells the complete story.
- First screenshot headline = the core value proposition (most important — often the only one seen).
- Subtitles add one specific detail or number below the headline.

## ASO Keyword Integration

Apple and Google now use visual intelligence to read text in screenshots and videos. Overlay text is indexed — making it a search optimization surface alongside the store listing metadata.

**Rules:**
1. **Natural inclusion.** Overlay text should naturally incorporate 2-3 high-value keywords from the extracted `keywords` list. Never keyword-stuff — the billboard test still applies.
2. **Prefer keyword phrasings.** When choosing between two equally good phrasings, prefer the one that includes a target keyword. "Track your reading habit" beats "Stay on top of your books" if "reading habit" is a keyword.
3. **Spread across scenes.** Distribute keywords across different captions/headlines rather than clustering them in one. Aim for at least 3 distinct keywords covered across the full set.
4. **Headlines > subtitles > captions.** Keywords in headlines carry more weight than subtitles, which carry more weight than video captions. Prioritize placement in the most prominent text.
5. **Don't sacrifice clarity.** A clear, benefit-first headline that misses a keyword is better than a keyword-stuffed headline that reads like SEO spam.

**Self-check:**
- [ ] Do the captions/headlines collectively cover at least 3 extracted keywords?
- [ ] Is every keyword used naturally — would a human copywriter write it this way?
- [ ] Are keywords spread across scenes, not clustered in one?

## Common Mistakes

- **"Your [Feature] Here"** — never use placeholder text. Always draft real copy, even if it needs revision.
- **Feature laundry lists** — don't cram 3 features into one caption. One feature, one line.
- **Repeating the app name in every caption** — mention it once (feature scene or CTA), not in every line.
- **Passive voice** — "Books can be logged quickly" → "Log any book in 5 seconds."
- **Starting with "We"** — the video is about the user, not the developer. "You" beats "We" every time.
